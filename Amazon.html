Experience Overview

During the summer of 2023, I interned at Amazon Web Services (AWS) within the User Experience organization. I joined a team building internal tools that measure and optimize user journeys across AWS pages — working alongside engineers, data scientists, and product managers in a fast-paced, data-driven environment.

My main project involved full-stack development in Scala, Spark, and TypeScript to detect and surface anomalies in the team's core analytics pipeline. The database underlying this tool would periodically lose data or produce inconsistent metrics, making it difficult for teams to trust their dashboards. I designed and implemented an anomaly detection algorithm that flagged meaningful discrepancies, integrating it with a variety of AWS services including S3, EC2, CloudWatch, and SageMaker.

The final deliverable was a monitoring dashboard that simplified debugging for the team — turning a complex investigation into a matter of checking which alarm was firing. I collaborated closely with teams across the UX organization to validate the approach and ensure the solution met real engineering needs.

Takeaways

  • Problem-Solving at Scale: This was my first taste of industry-scale engineering. I learned how to break down ambiguous problems, prototype solutions, and iterate quickly in a large production codebase with real user impact.
  • Cross-Team Communication: Working with multiple teams across the UX organization sharpened my ability to gather requirements, align on a solution, and communicate progress clearly to stakeholders at different levels.
