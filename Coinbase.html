Experience Overview

After graduating from Cornell University in May 2025, I joined Coinbase as a full-time Software Engineer, working across two teams — Transaction Intelligence (Financial Hub) and Consumer Post Trade. Both teams sit at the core of Coinbase's financial infrastructure, building Golang microservices focused on user gain/loss tracking and crypto portfolio management for millions of users.

My highest-impact contribution was shipping the Tax Lot Split and Selection APIs, which enabled accurate cost-basis tracking and flexible liquidation strategies for all Coinbase users. This was a significant financial feature that required careful design, precise implementation, and thorough testing given the sensitivity of tax-related data. I owned the full feature lifecycle — from technical design and Golang implementation through production rollout and observability.

In addition to feature work, I served as an on-call engineer, using Datadog dashboards to monitor service health and coordinating cross-team incident response to resolve user-impacting issues. This operational responsibility deepened my understanding of production systems and reinforced a reliability-first engineering mindset.

Takeaways

  • End-to-End Ownership: Owning the Tax Lot APIs from design to production taught me what it really means to be accountable for a feature — understanding not just the code, but the downstream impact on users and the business.
  • Reliability Engineering: On-call rotations gave me hands-on experience with production incident management — quickly diagnosing issues from dashboards, communicating clearly under pressure, and coordinating across teams to minimize user impact.
  • Cross-Team Collaboration: Working across two teams and engaging with engineers, product managers, and stakeholders across the organization sharpened my ability to build alignment and deliver in a complex, fast-moving environment.
