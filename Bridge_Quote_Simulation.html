Coinbase Bridging

Project Description

This was my main intern project during my Software Engineering Internship at Coinbase. I joined a fast-paced team of engineers on the Liquidity and Bridging subteam of the Blockchain Platform organization. The goal of my project was to build a tool that would simulate the cost and time of bridging assets from one blockchain to another through third-party options. This was a new initiative being explored to improve internal liquidity management and thus put my project at the front lines of innovation.

This project provided an answer to a fundamental problem that had been acting as a bottleneck to the Coinbase bridging infrastructure. There are certain common bridge transactions, such as bridging assets from Base to Ethereum, which require a 7-day challenge period due to the current protocols of the canonical bridge options being used. This challenge period essentially locks up these funds for a week, which takes a large toll on the liquidity of the company especially considering the large volumes being transferred and the overall volatility of crypto.

Third-party bridging offers a solution to this problem by eliminating the 7-day challenge period for a fee. This project evaluated the feasibility of replacing the current bridge options with third-party bridges by measuring the overall business impact of this change. This was one big learning experience that produced meaningful results, proving the potential to reduce the bridging delay of 7 days down to minutes!
