Tax Lot Split & Selection APIs

Coinbase SWE Project (2025 – 2026)

Cost-Basis Tracking at Coinbase

Tax lot accounting is how financial institutions track the cost basis of assets for tax reporting. In crypto, where assets are frequently bought, sold, and transferred across wallets and chains, accurate lot tracking is critical. Coinbase's Transaction Intelligence team owns this infrastructure — ensuring every user's gain/loss calculations are precise and compliant.

Project Description

The Tax Lot Split and Selection APIs were my primary project as a full-time Software Engineer on Coinbase's Transaction Intelligence team. Tax lots represent individual units of an asset acquired at a specific cost and time — and when a user liquidates crypto holdings, the selection of which lots to sell directly determines their taxable gain or loss.

I designed and implemented two core APIs: the Tax Lot Split API, which handles the division of existing lots when partial liquidations occur, and the Tax Lot Selection API, which enables flexible, user-configured strategies for choosing which lots to sell (e.g. FIFO, LIFO, highest-cost-first). Together, these APIs enabled accurate cost-basis tracking and flexible liquidation for all Coinbase users — a meaningful improvement to the financial accuracy of the platform.

I owned the full feature lifecycle — from technical design and API specification through Golang implementation, testing, production rollout, and post-launch observability via Datadog dashboards. Given the financial sensitivity of the feature, correctness and reliability were paramount throughout.

Takeaways

  • Financial Engineering Precision: Working on tax-sensitive infrastructure demands a level of correctness that goes beyond typical software. I developed strong habits around edge case analysis, thorough testing, and conservative rollout strategies.
  • API Design: Designing APIs consumed by multiple internal teams required careful thinking about contracts, backwards compatibility, and clear documentation — skills that will carry into every future project I work on.
