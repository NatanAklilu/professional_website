Experience Overview

After graduating from Cornell University in May 2025, I returned to Coinbase as a full-time Software Engineer on the Blockchain Platform team — the same team I had interned with the previous summer. This continuity allowed me to hit the ground running, contributing to production systems from day one with a deep understanding of the codebase and team culture.

My work centered on backend development in Golang, building and maintaining resilient systems that serve millions of users. Unlike my internship where I owned a single well-scoped project, full-time engineering meant taking on a much broader surface area — shipping features across multiple initiatives, participating in on-call rotations, reviewing code, and contributing to technical design decisions.

A defining aspect of this role was end-to-end feature ownership: taking a feature from initial design and scoping, through implementation, testing, and rollout, to post-launch observability and monitoring. Working in a high-growth environment meant operating with urgency while maintaining the engineering rigor that production infrastructure demands.

Takeaways

  • Production Mindset: Owning production systems instilled a strong sense of operational responsibility. Whether it was triaging an alert at odd hours or carefully reviewing a rollout plan, I learned what it means to truly own a service end-to-end.
  • Collaboration at Scale: Working cross-functionally with product managers, designers, and engineers across multiple teams sharpened my ability to communicate technical decisions to diverse audiences and align on shared goals.
  • Adaptability: The fast pace of a high-growth fintech company means priorities shift and new challenges emerge constantly. I grew significantly in my ability to context-switch effectively and deliver quality work under ambiguity.
