CS 3410: Computer System Organization and Programming

CS 3410 is one of Cornell's core Computer Science courses, covering computer organization, digital logic, processor design, and systems programming in C and RISC-V assembly. The course serves 300+ students per year and is a foundational requirement for Cornell's CS curriculum.

Role Overview

Throughout 2024, I served as a Teaching Assistant for CS 3410: Computer System Organization and Programming at Cornell's Ann S. Bowers College of Computing and Information Science. This was a year-long commitment spanning both the spring and fall semesters alongside my coursework and Coinbase internship.

I coordinated with professors to help shape the best possible learning experience for 300+ students in one of Cornell's most important core CS courses. My primary responsibilities included leading weekly lab sections and office hours, with 20+ students per session, helping them build a strong understanding of fundamental CS concepts ranging from digital logic and processor design to systems programming in C and RISC-V assembly.

Beyond direct student interaction, I proctored exams and graded all student submissions with actionable feedback — with the goal of ensuring every student understood not just their mistakes, but how to improve. This role deepened my own mastery of computer systems while developing skills in communication, mentorship, and teaching under time pressure.

Takeaways

  • Deep CS Foundations: Teaching CS 3410 required a thorough command of computer organization, memory hierarchies, and systems programming — reinforcing fundamentals that directly inform how I approach low-level engineering problems in my professional work.
  • Communication & Mentorship: Explaining complex topics clearly to students at different levels of understanding sharpened my ability to adapt explanations, meet people where they are, and make technical concepts accessible.
  • Time Management: Balancing a full TA workload with coursework and an internship demanded strong prioritization and discipline — a habit that has carried into my professional career.
